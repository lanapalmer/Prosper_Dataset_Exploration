Investigation Overview¶

In this project I will explore a data set provided by Prosper Marketplace.

Dataset Overview¶

Prosper Marketplace, Inc. is a privately-held, San Francisco-based company in the peer-to-peer lending industry. This dataset includes information on loans funded through the marketplace.

The ProsperScore: Average Score¶

The ProsperScore is a custom risk score built using historical Prosper data. Each borrower is assigned a score ranging from 1.0 (highest risk) to 10.0 (lowest risk). Lenders may use this score when choosing loans to fund. The next slide shows the range of ProsperScores, with an average of 5.86.

ProsperScore vs Lender Yield¶

The Lender Yield is equal to the interest rate on the loan less the Prosper servicing fee. As we see in the following slide, the lowest ranked borrowers, with a ProsperScore of 1.0, have a mean Lender Yield which is dramatically higher than the highest-ranked borrowers.

ProsperScore, Lender Yields and Investors¶

The number of investors for each loan in the dataset ranged from 1 to 1200, with an average of 80 investors per loan. The next slide shows the correlation between the number of investors, prosper score, and lender yield. We see that:

  • LenderYield and ProsperScore have a strong negative correlation (-0.64)

  • LenderYield and the number of Investors have a negative correction (-0.24)

  • The ProsperScore and the number of investors have a positive correlation (0.33)

Conclusion¶

This preliminary datset exploration has revealed investors who are willing to take the risk on borrowers with lower ProsperScores and fewer lenders are rewarded by higher returns. However, these borrowers are a rarity in the marketplace, with most borrowers falling in the 4.0-6.0 score range.
